A series of events to mark the seventieth anniversary of John Dillinger's
Tucson capture will kick off next weekend at the Downtown hotel where
the notorious gangster holed up in an attempt to escape the law.
  
"Dillinger Days: The Tucson Capture" rolls out next week at Hotel
Congress, with special musical performances, historical lectures and a
variety of historical memorabilia on display, including Dillinger's
tommy gun.
  
Local experts will be on hand next Friday to answer questions about
Dillinger's stay at Hotel Congress and the Tucson Police Department's
arrest of the nation's top wanted man. People can have their pictures
taken with Dillinger's tommy gun.  That night, the club will host the
Royal Crown Revue, described as the "kings of Los Angeles swing," by
the event organizers.  A historical docudrama is set for next
Saturday, re-creating the events leading to the capture of the
Dillinger gang. There will be three shows, a matinee, and two evening
shows.  Later, Hotel Congress will host a "Dillinger Ball," with food,
drinks and live thirties-era music. Tickets can be ordered online or
at the hotel. Proceeds will benefit the Tucson Police Foundation.
Dillinger was captured two days after a fire at the hotel forced him
and his gang into the open. One of the members paid a firefighter to
go back inside for a traveling bag in which police found pistols,
machine guns, ammunition and bulletproof vests.
